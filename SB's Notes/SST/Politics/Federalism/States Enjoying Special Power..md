## Asymmetrical Federalism in India

- Federations formed by **“holding together”** do not distribute power equally among all constituent units.
- In the **Indian Union**, states do not possess identical powers.
- Some states enjoy **special constitutional status** due to unique social and historical circumstances.

### States with Special Provisions

- States such as:
    - Assam
    - Nagaland
    - Arunachal Pradesh
    - Mizoram
- Receive special powers under certain provisions of the **Constitution of India (Article 371)**.

### Purpose of Special Powers

These provisions help protect:

- Land rights of indigenous communities
- Local cultures and traditions
- Preferential access to government employment

### Restrictions

- People who are not permanent residents of these states may be restricted from purchasing land or houses there.
- Similar special provisions also exist in some other Indian states.

## Union Territories

### What They Are

- Some areas of India are too small to become independent states and cannot be merged with existing states.
- Such areas are called **Union Territories**.

### Examples

- Chandigarh
- Lakshadweep
- Delhi
